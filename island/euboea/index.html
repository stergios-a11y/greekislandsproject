Euboea

Across the Chalkida bridge to the central plain Day 1, the thermal springs and quiet villages of the north Day 2, the rugged southern peaks and Karystos for departure Day 3.

Overall rating: 3.8/5 · 3684 km² · 210000 residents

Euboea (Evia in modern Greek) is 3,684 km² of mountains, gorges, thermal springs and an Aegean coastline that runs for 680 kilometres — and almost nobody outside Greece has heard of it. Connected to the mainland by a bridge at Chalkida, it's an easy 90-minute drive from Athens, no ferry required. The scale changes how you visit: you don't 'do' Euboea in a day. The south revolves around Karystos, Mount Ochi and the Dimosari Gorge; the centre around Chalkida and the alpine village of Steni below Mt. Dirfys (1,743m); the north around the thermal-springs town of Edipsos and the seaside villages of Limni and Agia Anna. Three days lets you sample one region per day. A week lets you start to know it.

Getting there

🚗 Drive 1h from Athens⛵ Rafina/Agia Marina too

Connected to mainland by bridge at Chalkida — drive from Athens, ~1h. Two ferry shortcuts also exist: Rafina–Marmari (south Evia, ~1h) and Agia Marina–Nea Styra (~30min).

Tip: For southern Evia (Karystos), the Marmari ferry beats the Chalkida drive every time.

3-day itinerary for Euboea

Day 1: Chalkida, Mt Dirfys & the East Coast

Overnight: Chalkida · Drive: 180 km, ~200 min

  1. 10:00 · Chalkida & the Euripus Tides
    The capital — 100,000 residents, lively waterfront, the famous Euripus tidal phenomenon: the current under the Old Bridge reverses direction every 6 hours and Aristotle reportedly died trying to explain why. Walk the bridge, watch the water flip.
  2. 11:30 · Karababa Castle
    Turk-Venetian fortress on a hill across the strait from Chalkida (Boeotian side). Free entry, panoramic views over the bridge, the strait, and Mount Dirfys to the east.
  3. 13:00 · Steni Village (Mt. Dirfys)
    30km east of Chalkida, in the alpine foothills of Mt. Dirfys (1,743m, the highest peak on Euboea). Cool mountain springs, plane-shaded squares, rustic tavernas serving wild boar and lamb. The trailhead for the Dirfys hike. Tavernas Kissos and Platanos for lunch.
  4. 15:00 · Chiliadou Beach
    1 hour northeast of Chalkida via a mountain road. Wild pebbly beach with emerald water, backed by green hills, with caves and a small church nearby. Featured in the 2022 film Triangle of Sadness. Some snack bars; otherwise unspoiled.
  5. 16:30 · Agia Anna Beach
    Long pebbly beach with crystal blue water and a backing pine forest, on the east coast about 1 hour north of Chiliadou. Cars park far from the sand, so the beach itself stays peaceful — the most natural-feeling beach on Evia.
  6. 20:30 · Chalkida — Evening
    Return to Chalkida for the night. Dinner on the Euripus waterfront — watch the famous tides reverse under the bridge.

Day 2: North — Limni, Lichadonisia & Edipsos Springs

Overnight: Edipsos · Drive: 140 km, ~160 min

  1. 10:30 · Limni Village
    Picture-perfect seaside village 40km southeast of Edipsos — terra-cotta-roofed houses, bougainvillea, narrow alleys spilling toward the sea. Coffee on the harbour, a quick walk through the lanes, then north to Edipsos.
  2. 13:30 · Edipsos Thermal Springs
    One of the oldest spa towns in Europe — Aristotle, Sulla, Hadrian, Greta Garbo, Winston Churchill, Onassis all came here. Free seaside hot pools where the spring water meets the Aegean (temperatures 30-86°C); upmarket Thermae Sylla Spa Hotel for a proper soak in neoclassical luxury. Arrive midday, drop bags, then continue west to Lichadonisia for the afternoon — return for the springs in the evening.
  3. 15:00 · Lichadonisia Islets
    20 minutes west of Edipsos at Loutra Gialtron. A small archipelago accessible only by boat — uninhabited islets with shallow turquoise water that some travel writers have called 'the Greek Maldives.' Half-day boat trips from Gialtra harbour.
  4. 19:00 · Edipsos Thermal Spa — Evening Soak
    Back at Edipsos for the main event — the open-air thermal pools where the hot mineral water meets the sea. Dinner in town; the spa hotels here have pools that stay open late.

Day 3: South — Karystos, Dragon Houses & Departure

Drive: 260 km, ~290 min

  1. 08:00 · Edipsos → Karystos
    The long drive south — about 210 km, almost the full length of the island. Coffee at the start, take it slow on the central mountain road. Stop at Eretria along the way to break up the transit.
  2. 10:30 · Ancient Eretria
    Important classical city-state from the 9th century BC, mentioned by Homer as one of the cities sending ships to Troy. The site is right on the road south, ~10 km past Chalkida — a 45-minute stop breaks up the long transit.
  3. 12:30 · Karystos
    Easygoing seaside town on the southern shore — long sandy beach, sunny central square lined with cafés, the 14th-century Venetian Bourtzi tower right on the waterfront. Ferry arrivals from Marmari (next door) connect to Rafina near Athens.
  4. 13:30 · Castello Rosso
    11th-century Venetian-Frankish castle on a hillside above Karystos, overlooking the Gulf of Karystos with Mt. Ochi as backdrop. Imposing red-walled ruin, free entry, panoramic views.
  5. 15:00 · Dragon Houses (Drakospita)
    23 rectangular megalithic structures built of limestone slabs, attributed to the pre-Hellenic Dryopes around the 5th century BC. The largest sits at the summit of Mt. Ochi (1,398m) — its 10-ton roof slabs are an unsolved engineering mystery. Reach the easier sites at Palli Lakka, near Styra.
  6. 16:00 · Dimosari Gorge Hike
    10km, 4-hour descent from the highlands of Mt. Ochi to the sea — too long for the departure day, but worth driving up to the trailhead viewpoint for a short walk and the panorama back over Karystos. Return to Karystos for an early dinner.
  7. 17:30 · Dinner — Cavo d'Oro
    Karystos taverna doing southern Euboean home cooking — octopus in red wine, grilled halloumi, the local spanakorizo (rice with spinach). Early dinner before the ferry.
  8. 19:30 · Marmari Ferry — Departure
    Drive 10km west to Marmari port. Ferry to Rafina takes about 1 hour — much faster than driving back. Several departures daily; check schedules on Ferryhopper.

Top beaches of Euboea

Chiliadou Beach

The most famous beach on Euboea — wild pebbly stretch with emerald water, green hills behind, sea caves nearby. Featured in Ruben Östlund's 2022 film Triangle of Sadness. Less developed than its fame suggests, still mostly the preserve of Greek visitors.

Type
Sand and small pebble
Length
500 m
Depth
Medium — clear emerald water
Facing
East-facing — open to the Aegean
Facilities
Light: snack bars, tavernas, small church and caves nearby. 1-hour mountain drive from Chalkida.

Agia Anna Beach

Long northeastern coast beach with deep blue water and a pine forest behind. The car park separation from the sand keeps it feeling unspoiled even in August. The default beach for the north coast and the best camping option on the island.

Type
Pebble and sand
Length
1.5 km
Depth
Medium — gradual entry
Facing
Northeast-facing — fronted by Aegean
Facilities
Good: beach bars, tavernas, popular campsite in pine forest. Cars park far from sand.

Agia Paraskevi Beach (Karystos)

Soft sand and exceptionally clear water on the south coast, west of Karystos town. The drive is hairpin-curvy but the beach earns it — calm, family-friendly, the kind of water that makes you want to spend the whole day.

Type
Soft sand
Length
300 m
Depth
Shallow to medium — exceptionally clear water
Facing
South-facing — sheltered
Facilities
Light: a couple of tavernas, sunbeds. Reached via hairpin road from Karystos.

Kallianos Beach

Secluded pebble beach at the bottom of the Dimosari Gorge — the reward swim after a 4-hour mountain descent. The water is cold (mountain runoff) but blissfully clear. Quiet even in August because most visitors don't make the hike.

Type
Pebble
Length
200 m
Depth
Medium — clear, deep enough to swim
Facing
East-facing
Facilities
Minimal — a small canteen. Reached at the end of the Dimosari Gorge hike or by road.
