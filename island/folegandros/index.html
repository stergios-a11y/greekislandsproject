Folegandros

The clifftop Chora, the church of Panagia, the beach at Angali and the wild west coast.

Overall rating: 4.0/5 · 32 km² · 765 residents

Folegandros is what the Cyclades looked like before mass tourism — a tiny, steep island with no cars in the main village, a clifftop Chora that is among the most beautiful in Greece, and beaches accessible only on foot or by boat. It has been quietly popular with discerning travellers for decades. The population is 650. Everything moves at walking pace. Come here to disconnect completely.

3-day itinerary for Folegandros

Day 1: Chora & Panagia

Overnight: Chora · Drive: 5 km, ~10 min

  1. 09:30 · Chora (Folegandros Town)
    One of the finest Cycladic villages in Greece — a medieval kastro and a series of interconnected squares with no cars, excellent tavernas and bougainvillea everywhere. Walk through all three squares.
  2. 11:00 · Church of Panagia
    The church on the clifftop above the Chora — accessed by a steep path, with views over sheer 200m cliffs to the sea below. Carried to the cliff every August in the island's great festival.
  3. 14:30 · Karavostasis Beach
    The port beach — small, pebble, with good tavernas. Not spectacular but convenient, with a good fish restaurant directly on the waterfront.

Day 2: Angali & the West

Overnight: Chora · Drive: 8 km, ~12 min

  1. 10:00 · Angali Beach
    The best beach on Folegandros — a sandy cove accessible by a 20-minute walk or the island minibus. Clear water, excellent small taverna, relaxed atmosphere. The path continues west to more secluded coves.
  2. 12:30 · Agios Nikolaos Beach
    Wild pebble beach a 15-minute walk west of Angali — completely empty, no facilities, extraordinary water clarity. The most secluded accessible beach on the island.
  3. 19:00 · Chora Evening
    Return to Chora for sunset at the kastro walls, then dinner at one of the village square tavernas. Folegandros has the best taverna food of any small Cycladic island.

Day 3: Ano Meria & Departure

Overnight: · Drive: 10 km, ~15 min

  1. 09:30 · Ano Meria Village
    The second village of Folegandros — a scattered settlement of farms and windmills across the plateau. The folk museum here is one of the finest on the small Cyclades.
  2. 11:30 · Livadaki Beach
    Remote beach accessible only on foot (45 min from Ano Meria) or by boat. Wild, empty, exceptional water. For those willing to walk.
  3. 16:00 · Karavostasis — Departure
    Ferry to Piraeus, Santorini, Milos or Sikinos. Folegandros has fewer connections than larger islands — check timetables carefully.

Top beaches of Folegandros

Angali Beach

The best beach on Folegandros — a sandy cove with clear water and one of the finest small tavernas in the Cyclades. The walk from Chora through the fields adds to the experience. Everything about it is simple and perfect.

Type
Sand and fine pebble
Length
200 m
Depth
Medium — clear Aegean water
Facing
West-facing — open sea, calm in summer
Facilities
Basic: excellent small taverna, no sunbeds. 20-minute walk or minibus from Chora.

Agios Nikolaos Beach

Wild, deserted pebble cove west of Angali. The most secluded beach on the island — almost always empty. Exceptional water clarity and complete silence.

Type
Pebble
Length
150 m
Depth
Deep — crystal clear
Facing
West-facing — open sea
Facilities
None — 15-minute walk from Angali.
