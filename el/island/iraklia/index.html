Ηρακλειά

The village of Agios Georgios, Livadi beach, and the hike to Tourkopigado.

Συνολική βαθμολογία: 3.3/5 · 18 km² · 140 κάτοικοι

Iraklia is the first stop on the Express Skopelitis ferry south from Naxos — a tiny island of 140 residents with a single port, two small beaches near the village, and a third (one of the finest in the Cyclades) reached only on foot. The Cave of Agios Ioannis is the largest cave in the Cyclades. The island has almost no tourist development: a handful of rooms to let, two or three tavernas, no hotels. For travellers who want the authentic Small Cyclades experience without spending a full week.

Δρομολόγιο 1 ημερών — Ηρακλειά

Μέρα 1: Village, Beach & Cave

Διανυκτέρευση: Agios Georgios · Οδήγηση: 12 km, ~45 min

  1. 09:00 · Agios Georgios (Port)
    The only village and port — 140 residents, two tavernas, a bakery, a small church. Arrive by Express Skopelitis from Naxos (1.5 hrs) or Amorgos. The village is immediately behind the harbour.
  2. 10:00 · Livadi Beach
    The closest beach to the village — a short walk south. Fine sand, crystal clear shallow water, a handful of sunbeds and a simple beach bar. One of the most unaffected family beaches in the Cyclades.
  3. 13:00 · Lunch — Village Taverna
    The village tavernas cook what the local fishermen bring in. Simple, excellent, a third of Mykonos prices. The owner probably knows everyone on the island by name.
  4. 15:00 · Tourkopigado Beach
    The finest beach on Iraklia — a wild south-coast bay with fine pebbles and extraordinary water clarity. Reached by a 40-minute walking path from the village. No facilities, total peace.
  5. 17:00 · Cave of Agios Ioannis
    The largest cave in the Cyclades — a two-chamber cave with stalactites, a chapel at the entrance, and remarkable acoustic properties. Access is only on foot from a trailhead near Panagia. Guided visits on August 29 (local feast day).
  6. 18:00 · Departure or 2nd Night
    Skopelitis back to Naxos, onward to Schinoussa, Koufonisia, Donousa and Amorgos. A second night is worth it if you've made the journey this far.
